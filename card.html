The bald eagle only appears in North America, and is the national bird of the U.S.A.

  • Scientific Name: Haliaeetus Leucocepalus
  • Average Wingspan: 7 feet
  • Weight Range: 6 to 14 pounds
  • Average Lifespan: 20 to 30 years

The bald eagle was on the brink of extinction in the later 1900s but has since fully recovered. Bald Eagles make the largest nests of all birds, and often resides near bodies of water where it can catch its favorite food, fish. Females grow up to 25% larger than males!
